Overview
ShiftEasy
A construction worker shift tracking app. Workers start and stop shifts from their phone. Managers see live timesheets, reports, and manage their entire crew from mobile or desktop. Built to replace informal Telegram tracking with a secure, server-verified system.

Multiple sites — smart picker
When a worker is assigned to 2 or more sites, tapping Start shows a bottom sheet with all their sites listed. They select one and the confirm button updates dynamically — "Start shift on Block A". History — three views, one pattern
All three views use the same interaction pattern — compact rows with a mini bar chart showing relative shift length, and a tap to expand inline details. No separate detail page needed.
  • This week — always 7 rows (Mon–Sun). Days with no shift show a dash. Days off are visually dimmed.
  • This month — only days that had a shift are shown. No empty rows. Scrollable for the full month.
  • All time — grouped by month. One row per month showing total hours and shift count. Tap a month → drills down to that month's day list. Back button returns to the month list.
  • The bar next to each row gives a visual feel of shift length without reading the number.

Profile — identity only, no stats
The profile page is intentionally minimal. No shift stats are shown here — hours and shift counts live in the History tab. Profile serves one purpose: showing who you are and giving access to account settings.
  • Avatar — initials only, no photos. Keeps it clean and professional.
  • Active badge — shows role and assigned site at a glance.
  • Account section — email, company, manager name, member since. All read-only for the worker.
  • Settings — change password and notifications toggle. That's all the worker can control.
  • Sign out — red button at the bottom, clearly destructive.

Desktop shifts — two views + detail drawer
The desktop Shifts page has two views accessible from the top bar.
  • Timesheet view — Remato-style grid. Workers on the left, days across the top. Today highlighted in blue. Each cell shows hours, delta (+/-), and time range. Active shifts show in green with a live dot. Special states: Vacation in amber, Sick leave in red. Totals row at the bottom.
  • Detail drawer — clicking any cell slides a panel in from the right. Grid narrows to make space. Drawer shows worker avatar, full duration, exact start/end timestamps, site, materials, and full notes. Click ✕ to close.
  • List view — one row per shift, sortable. Better when filtering to one specific worker. Shows worker, date, time, notes preview, and duration. Active shifts highlighted in green. Total shown at the bottom.
  • Export button — dropdown with Download CSV and Download PDF options.

  • No profile photos — initials only throughout the app.

Manager profile — access via avatar tap
The manager profile is not in the bottom nav — it's accessed by tapping the SC avatar in the top right of the Dashboard. This keeps the nav to 4 clean items (Dashboard, Shifts, Workers, Sites) and follows the same pattern used by apps like Instagram and Slack.
  • 2 stats at top — sites managed and total workers. Quick overview of responsibility.
  • Account — email (read-only) and member since date.
  • Company settings — edit company name and invite another manager to the account.
  • Settings — change password and notifications toggle.
  • Sign out — red button, clearly destructive.

Desktop profile — identity panel + settings panel
The desktop profile is a two-column layout. Left panel is identity — avatar, name, role, company badge, stats, sites managing list, sign out. Right panel is settings grouped into clear sections.
  • Account — email, member since, role. All read-only.
  • Company — edit company name inline, invite another manager by email.
  • Security — change password link.
  • Preferences — email notifications toggle, language selector.
  • Danger zone — delete company account. Red text, clearly destructive action.
